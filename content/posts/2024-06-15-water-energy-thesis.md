The next decade will be defined by how we manage water and energy resources. I've spent the last several years thinking deeply about this space, and I want to share my framework for evaluating opportunities.

## The Core Belief

Water and energy are fundamental. They underpin every economic activity, yet we've historically underpriced and mismanaged both. The correction is coming, and it will create enormous opportunities for those positioned correctly.

## What I Look For

When evaluating companies and projects in this space, I focus on:

1. **Market mechanisms** — Solutions that create or improve markets for natural resources
2. **Measurability** — Technologies that enable better tracking and verification
3. **Scalability** — Approaches that can expand without proportional cost increases
4. **Regulatory alignment** — Businesses that work with, not against, the policy direction

## The Opportunity

Environmental markets are still in their infancy. Water quality trading, carbon markets, and renewable energy certificates are just the beginning. The infrastructure layer — the pipes, sensors, software, and marketplaces — represents the real opportunity.
