I often say I'm focused on "building stronger businesses." But what does that actually mean? Here's my working definition.

## Strength vs. Scale

A strong business isn't necessarily a large one. Strength is about resilience — the ability to survive and thrive through changing conditions. It's about:

- **Unit economics that work** — Every transaction should be profitable on its own
- **Multiple revenue streams** — Diversification protects against single points of failure
- **Customer relationships, not transactions** — Repeat business is more valuable than one-time sales
- **Low fixed costs** — Flexibility to scale up or down as conditions require

## The Long Game

I'm not interested in building businesses to flip. I'm interested in building businesses that generate sustainable returns for decades. This requires different thinking:

- Prioritize cash flow over growth metrics
- Build real assets, not just user bases
- Focus on markets with genuine demand, not manufactured hype

## Why This Matters

In a world obsessed with unicorns and blitzscaling, I think there's enormous opportunity in boring, profitable businesses that solve real problems. They may not make headlines, but they make money — and they last.
