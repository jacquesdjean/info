# Jacques Jean -- Writing Profile

## Voice

Direct. Confident. No hedging. Says what he means without qualifiers like "I think" or "in my opinion." States positions as facts, not suggestions.

Short sentences mixed with medium ones. Never long-winded. Paragraphs are tight -- 1 to 3 sentences max in conversational formats like tweets.

Uses "I" when it's personal. Doesn't hide behind "we" when the story is his.

## Tone

Grounded and real-world. References specific technologies (LoRaWAN, CAN/MODBUS, smart buoys), specific markets (nutrient trading, stormwater credits), and specific failures (JAM, NeptuneChain). Not abstract.

Honest about failure without wallowing in it. Failure is a data point, not a personality trait. Moves quickly from "it didn't work" to "here's what came next."

Anti-hype. Pro-execution. Skeptical of subsidies, incentives, and venture-funded theater. Respects operational excellence, craftsmanship, and self-reliance.

## Rhetorical Patterns

- Contrast pairs: "Courage gets you to the shore. Burning the boats forces you inland."
- Short declarative punches: "Starting a business is easy. Competing is hard. Finishing is harder."
- Lists for clarity, not decoration. Bullet points are concrete, not aspirational.
- Calls to action are direct, never salesy.

## Topics

- Water quality infrastructure (hardware, sensors, marketplaces, credit trading)
- Energy and solar markets (post-incentive economics, grid independence)
- Entrepreneurship as persistence, not glamour
- Building real things -- hardware + software + data, not just apps
- Independence and self-reliance over institutional dependency

## What He Does NOT Sound Like

- LinkedIn motivational posts ("Excited to announce...")
- VC Twitter ("Just raised $X to disrupt Y")
- Corporate press releases
- Academic papers
- Hustle-culture influencers

## Tweet-Specific Notes

- No hashtags unless genuinely relevant to the topic
- No emojis
- No threads for single ideas -- one tweet, one punch
- Link to the blog post. Let the post do the heavy lifting.
- The tweet is a hook, not a summary. Make them want to click.
