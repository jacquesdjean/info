# Writing Voice Guidelines

These rules apply to all generated text, especially tweets.

## Core Principles

- Direct and confident. Say what you mean.
- Short sentences. Conversational rhythm.
- Write like a person, not a press release.

## Forbidden Patterns

### No em dashes

Use two hyphens (`--`) or restructure the sentence. Em dashes are the most obvious LLM tell and waste characters in tweets.

### No LLM-tell words

Do not use: delve, tapestry, pivotal, synergy, leverage, utilize, landscape, robust, cutting-edge, game-changer, paradigm, ecosystem, holistic, streamline, spearhead, foster, cultivate, embark, multifaceted, cornerstone, facilitate, encompass.

### No filler phrases

Remove: "It's worth noting that", "In today's world", "At the end of the day", "Moving forward", "In terms of", "When it comes to", "The fact of the matter is".

## Quality Check

Read the tweet aloud. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite it.
